Cognition

The Mind of an Octopus

Peter Godfrey-Smith, January 1 2018. Scientific American (No bias studies done)

Octopus brains are completely different from ours, with a piece of it in each tentacle. However, they exhibit personality, reasoning, the ability to recognize faces and perhaps even self awareness. An interesting look into what it means to be conscious and how cognition works in other animals.



Future Tech

How Google's Quantum Computer Could Change the World

Jack Nicas, October 16, 2017. Wall Street Journal (Center)

A general explanation of quantum computing. Interesting connection to parallel universes. Description of Google's computer. Useful article if you are looking for an intro to the issues with quantum computing.



A.I.

Protecting Against AI's Existential Threat

Ilya Sutskever and Dario Amodei, October 18, 2017. Wall Street Journal (Center)

Excellent analysis of the ethics problems with AI. Discusses an example where an AI exploits a glitch in a game to rack up points by crashing into walls. Draws parallels to real life AI. Who is accountable for the failures of an computer program?



Sex Differences

Two minds: The cognitive differences between men and women

Bruce Goldman, Spring 2017. Stanford Medicine (No bias studies done)

An extremely factual approach to a nuanced and inflammatory topic. The author makes interesting arguments and actively disproves many counterclaims.



Green

Fresh, strong arguments about climate change. A welcome departure from platitudes.
